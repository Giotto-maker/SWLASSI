The four temples, originally designated by the letters A, B, C, and D, 
front onto a paved street. The area was delineated to the North by the Hecatostylum 
and the Baths of Agrippa, and to the South by Circus Flaminius, 
to the East by porched square of Porticus Minucia Frumentaria, 
and to the West by the Theatre of Pompey
